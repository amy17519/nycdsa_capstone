In general, the output of ParseJSON is a list of json objects, represented by a NESTED dictionary. To access the average stars and business categories of each business, we

stars = ProcessID(data_business,'stars')
# stars is a list of numbers

# stars[:10]
#[4.5, 3.5, 5.0, 2.5, 2.5, 5.0, 2.5, 4.0, 2.5, 4.0]

categories = ProcessID(data_business,'categories')
# Because each business can belong to more than one category (alphebatically sorted),
# categories is a list of lists of strings.

ProcessID peels off the data layer by layer, similar to beautiful soup. Based on this technique, we find that

  • basic info on the review file: 2225213 reviews, 3798 different dates (about 10 years), 5 different star level, 77079 distinct business IDs, 552339 different usersID, only 1 type of review (so type seems to be redundant)

  • check in data has 55569 unique business ID. So each line is a different business

  • The business location covers 29 different states. 66878 are still open, 10567 are closed

  • There are 892 different business categories in the data set.
